FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our hearing care services and what to expect during your visit.

If you find yourself frequently asking people to repeat themselves, turning up the TV volume, or having difficulty following conversations in noisy environments, it may be time for a hearing assessment. We recommend annual hearing tests for adults over 50, or sooner if you notice any changes in your hearing.

A comprehensive hearing assessment typically takes 60-90 minutes. We'll review your medical history, examine your ears, and conduct various tests to measure your hearing ability across different frequencies. We'll then discuss the results with you and recommend appropriate solutions if needed.

Coverage varies depending on your insurance provider and policy. Some private health insurance plans offer partial rebates for hearing aids. We can help you understand your coverage options and work with your insurance provider to maximise your benefits.

The adjustment period varies for each individual, but most people adapt to their new hearing aids within 2-4 weeks. We provide ongoing support and follow-up appointments to ensure your hearing aids are properly fitted and programmed for optimal comfort and performance.

We recommend having your hearing aids professionally cleaned and serviced every 6 months. Regular maintenance helps ensure optimal performance and extends the lifespan of your devices. We also provide daily care instructions to help you maintain your hearing aids at home.

Yes, we understand that some clients may have mobility challenges or prefer the comfort of their own home. We offer home visit services for hearing assessments and hearing aid fittings. Please contact us to discuss your needs and schedule an appointment.

HEARING BENEFITS

Experience the Benefits of Better Hearing

Discover how improved hearing can transform your daily life and reconnect you with the world around you.

Improved Communication

Enjoy clearer conversations with family and friends, reducing miscommunication and strengthening relationships through better understanding.

Enhanced Cognitive Function

Studies show that treating hearing loss can help maintain mental sharpness and may reduce the risk of cognitive decline.

Increased Confidence

Feel more confident in social situations, at work, and in public spaces when you can hear clearly and participate fully in conversations.

Better Safety Awareness

Stay alert to important sounds like alarms, traffic, and emergency signals, ensuring your safety in all environments.

Active Social Life

Participate more actively in social gatherings, enjoy entertainment, and maintain meaningful connections with others.

Reduced Listening Fatigue

Experience less mental exhaustion from straining to hear, allowing you to enjoy conversations without feeling drained.
