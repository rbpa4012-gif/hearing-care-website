## CONTENT WRITING FRAMEWORK

### Article Structure Template

```markdown
# [Article Title - Include Primary Keyword]

## Introduction (150-200 words)
- Hook: Start with a relatable scenario or startling statistic
- Problem identification: What issue does this article address?
- Value proposition: What will readers learn?
- Disclaimer: "This article is for educational purposes only. Always consult with a qualified audiologist for personalized advice."

## Main Content Sections (1,500-2,000+ words total)

### Section 1: [Topic Foundation]
- Define key terms
- Explain basic concepts
- Use analogies for complex ideas

### Section 2: [Detailed Information]
- Provide comprehensive coverage
- Include statistics (with sources)
- Add examples relevant to Central Queensland

### Section 3: [Practical Application]
- How this applies to daily life
- Tips and recommendations
- What to expect from professional consultation

### Section 4: [Local Context]
- Relevance to Rockhampton/CQ residents
- Available local services at CQ Audiology
- Regional considerations (climate, lifestyle)

## Key Takeaways (Bullet Points)
- 5-7 main points summarizing the article
- Action items for readers
- When to seek professional help

## Frequently Asked Questions
Q1: [Common question related to topic]
A1: [Comprehensive answer 100-150 words]

Q2: [Another relevant question]
A2: [Detailed response with practical advice]

Q3: [Local or service-specific question]
A3: [Answer that naturally mentions CQ Audiology services]

## Call to Action
"If you're experiencing [relevant symptoms/concerns], the team at CQ Audiology is here to help. Book your comprehensive hearing assessment today by calling (07) 4848 6528 or visiting our Rockhampton clinic."

## Meta Information
- Meta Title: [50-60 characters including keyword]
- Meta Description: [150-160 characters with compelling CTA]
- Focus Keyword: [Primary keyword phrase]
- Secondary Keywords: [2-3 related terms]
- URL Slug: /blog/[keyword-focused-url]
```

## HEARING HEALTH COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK

*Version 1.0 - 16 Rules for Australian Healthcare Content Compliance*
*Aligned with AHPRA, TGA, and ACCC Regulations*

### Medical Content Do's and Don'ts

#### DO:
- Use phrases like "may help," "could improve," "often beneficial"
- Include disclaimers about seeking professional advice
- Reference credible sources (WHO, Hearing Australia, government sites)
- Focus on education and awareness
- Describe general processes and procedures
- Share general statistics and research findings

#### DON'T:
- Make specific medical claims or guarantees
- Provide diagnosis or treatment recommendations
- Use terms like "cure," "heal," or "guaranteed results"
- Give specific medical advice
- Make comparisons claiming superiority over competitors
- Quote specific success rates without context
- Make assumptions about the reader's condition or situation
- Make unverified claims or statements presented as fact
- Offer guarantees of outcomes, results, or satisfaction
- Use absolute language like "always," "never," "definitely," "certainly," "will"
- Present opinions or possibilities as certainties

#### STRICTLY PROHIBITED CONTENT:

**1. Home Health Remedies & Self-Treatment Advice:**
- NO instructions for cleaning ears or removing earwax at home
- NO DIY hearing care procedures or techniques
- NO home remedies for tinnitus, ear infections, or hearing issues
- NO "natural" or alternative treatment suggestions
- Always direct readers to seek professional care for any ear/hearing concerns

**2. Nutritional & Dietary Claims:**
- NO claims that specific foods improve hearing health
- NO vitamin or supplement recommendations for hearing
- NO dietary advice linked to hearing preservation or improvement
- NO "eat this to prevent hearing loss" type content
- Avoid any nutritional guidance - this is outside audiologist scope of practice

**3. Pricing & Dollar Values:**
- NO specific dollar amounts for services (e.g., "$150 hearing test")
- NO specific dollar amounts for products (e.g., "$2,000 hearing aids")
- NO price ranges (e.g., "$1,500-$5,000")
- NO percentage discounts or savings claims (e.g., "Save 20%")
- Use general terms instead: "competitive pricing," "various price points," "funding options available," "contact us for pricing"
- OK to mention FREE services if genuinely free (e.g., "free initial consultation")
- OK to reference government funding programs by name without dollar amounts

**4. Competitor References:**
- NO naming competitor businesses
- NO price comparisons with competitors
- NO quality comparisons with specific competitors
- NO "cheaper than" or "better than" statements

**5. Unproven Health Condition Links:**
- NO claims linking other health conditions to hearing loss without peer-reviewed scientific evidence
- NO stating conditions like dementia, diabetes, or cardiovascular disease "cause" or "affect" hearing
- NO implying correlation equals causation between health conditions and hearing
- NO referencing studies or research unless from peer-reviewed, reputable medical journals
- NO sensationalist health connections (e.g., "dementia is affecting your hearing")
- If discussing potential associations, use cautious language: "some research suggests a possible association" and always recommend consulting healthcare professionals
- Stick to established, well-documented causes of hearing loss (noise exposure, age, genetics, physical trauma, infections)

**6. Testimonials & Reviews (AHPRA Regulated):**
- NO patient testimonials implying specific health outcomes
- NO before/after claims or comparisons
- NO "success stories" that suggest guaranteed or typical results
- NO fabricated, incentivised, or unverifiable reviews
- NO cherry-picked positive reviews without balanced representation
- If using testimonials, they must be genuine, verifiable, and not imply clinical outcomes
- Focus on service experience (e.g., "friendly staff") rather than health results

**7. Qualifications & Credentials:**
- NO exaggerating staff qualifications or experience
- NO implying specialisations not formally held or registered
- NO misleading use of titles (e.g., "Dr" without relevant medical/doctoral degree)
- NO claiming memberships or affiliations that don't exist or have lapsed
- Always accurately represent professional credentials as registered with AHPRA
- Verify all qualifications before publishing

**8. Urgency & Fear-Based Marketing:**
- NO scare tactics (e.g., "Act now or lose your hearing forever")
- NO artificial urgency (e.g., "Limited time only", "Only 3 spots left", "Offer ends soon")
- NO fear-mongering about hearing loss progression or consequences
- NO guilt-inducing language targeting family members (e.g., "Don't let your loved ones suffer")
- NO countdown timers or false scarcity tactics
- NO catastrophising language about untreated hearing loss
- Content should inform and empower, not frighten or pressure

**9. Age & Disability Sensitivity:**
- NO language suggesting elderly people "should" or "need to" get hearing aids
- NO implying hearing loss is shameful, embarrassing, or a sign of decline
- NO patronising or condescending language about age-related conditions
- NO stereotyping based on age, disability, or hearing status
- NO language that undermines autonomy or decision-making capacity
- Respect dignity, independence, and personal choice in all content
- Use person-first language where appropriate (e.g., "person with hearing loss" not "hearing impaired person")

**10. Third-Party Claims & Endorsements:**
- NO claiming endorsements from organisations without written permission
- NO implying government endorsement (Medicare, NDIS, Hearing Australia) without authorisation
- NO using logos of government bodies, medical associations, or funding bodies without permission
- NO celebrity or influencer endorsements without proper disclosure
- NO implying affiliation with hospitals, universities, or research institutions without formal relationship
- NO fake or misleading partnership claims
- All endorsements must be genuine, current, and properly disclosed

**11. Product & Brand References:**
- NO favouring specific hearing aid brands without disclosing commercial arrangements
- NO disparaging other brands, products, or technologies
- NO presenting sponsored or paid content as independent editorial advice
- NO making comparative claims about brand superiority
- NO claiming exclusive access to products available elsewhere
- If brand partnerships exist, clearly disclose the relationship
- Present product information factually without promotional bias

**12. Social Media & User-Generated Content:**
- Must monitor and moderate comments on all social platforms
- Remove any comments that could constitute prohibited testimonials or outcome claims
- Responsible for third-party endorsements and influencer content posted on business pages
- Cannot allow outcome-based reviews to remain visible on owned platforms
- User comments claiming health outcomes must be removed or hidden
- Influencer partnerships must be clearly disclosed as paid/sponsored content
- Do not reshare patient posts that imply treatment outcomes

**13. Before-and-After Imagery:**
- NO before/after photos implying treatment or hearing improvement outcomes
- If imagery is used, must be unfiltered and standardised with identical lighting/positioning
- Cannot imply guaranteed, expected, or typical results through imagery
- NO edited, filtered, or enhanced comparison images
- Avoid visual representations that suggest transformation or dramatic improvement
- Stock photos must not mislead about actual service outcomes

**14. Vulnerable Groups Protection:**
- Extra caution required with content targeting elderly populations
- NO exploiting concerns, fears, or emotions of family members/carers
- NO targeting people with serious health conditions with pressure tactics
- Avoid content that could be seen as preying on vulnerability or desperation
- NO suggesting that declining hearing services is harmful to family relationships
- Content must empower decision-making, not manipulate vulnerable individuals
- Be mindful of cognitive accessibility in all communications

**15. Inducements & Offers Transparency:**
- Any promotional offers must have transparent, clearly stated terms and conditions
- NO hidden conditions, exclusions, or fine print on promotions
- Free consultations must be genuinely free with no obligation or pressure to purchase
- Discount terms must be clearly stated upfront (eligibility, duration, exclusions)
- NO bait-and-switch tactics (advertising one offer, delivering another)
- Time-limited offers must have genuine end dates, not rolling "limited time" claims
- Bundled offers must clearly state what is and isn't included

**16. Children & Paediatric Services:**
- Extra sensitivity required when discussing paediatric hearing services
- NO marketing pressure tactics directed at parents or guardians
- NO advertising that targets children directly
- Content about children's hearing must be purely educational and informative
- Avoid language that induces parental guilt or fear about children's hearing
- Respect parental autonomy in healthcare decisions for their children
- Always recommend appropriate paediatric specialists where relevant

### Example Compliant Language

**Instead of:** "Our hearing aids will cure your tinnitus"
**Write:** "Many people with tinnitus find relief through properly fitted hearing devices, though individual results vary"

**Instead of:** "You definitely have hearing loss if you experience these symptoms"
**Write:** "These symptoms may indicate hearing changes that warrant professional assessment"

**Instead of:** "CQ Audiology is the best audiologist in Queensland"
**Write:** "CQ Audiology is committed to providing comprehensive hearing care to the Central Queensland community"

**Instead of:** "Clean your ears at home using cotton buds or ear candles"
**Write:** "Earwax removal should always be performed by a qualified professional to avoid damage to the ear canal"

**Instead of:** "Eat more leafy greens and fish to improve your hearing"
**Write:** "A healthy lifestyle may support overall wellbeing - speak with your GP about general health advice"

**Instead of:** "Our hearing aids start from $1,500"
**Write:** "We offer hearing solutions at various price points - contact our team to discuss options that suit your budget"

**Instead of:** "Save $500 on hearing aids this month"
**Write:** "Ask about our current offers when you book your consultation"

**Instead of:** "Try these home remedies for tinnitus relief"
**Write:** "If you're experiencing tinnitus, book a professional assessment to explore evidence-based management options"

**Instead of:** "You will notice improvement within weeks"
**Write:** "Many people report noticing changes over time, though experiences vary"

**Instead of:** "This hearing aid will solve your problems"
**Write:** "This style of hearing aid may be suitable for certain types of hearing loss"

**Instead of:** "Your hearing loss is definitely caused by noise exposure"
**Write:** "Noise exposure is one possible factor that a professional assessment can help identify"

**Instead of:** "We guarantee you'll be satisfied"
**Write:** "We're committed to working with you to find the right solution"

**Instead of:** "Dementia causes hearing loss" or "Hearing loss leads to dementia"
**Write:** "Some research explores potential associations between cognitive health and hearing - consult your healthcare team for personalised advice"

**Instead of:** "Diabetes is damaging your hearing"
**Write:** "Various factors can contribute to hearing changes - a comprehensive assessment can help identify your individual situation"

**Instead of:** "Studies prove that heart disease affects your ears"
**Write:** "Maintaining overall health is important - speak with your GP about any health concerns"

**Instead of:** "John got his hearing aids and now he can hear perfectly again!"
**Write:** "Our clients appreciate our personalised approach and supportive care environment"

**Instead of:** "Our lead audiologist is the most experienced in Queensland"
**Write:** "Our audiologists hold current AHPRA registration and relevant qualifications"

**Instead of:** "Act NOW before your hearing gets worse! Limited appointments available!"
**Write:** "We welcome you to book a consultation at a time that suits you"

**Instead of:** "Don't let grandma struggle - get her the hearing aids she needs"
**Write:** "If you or a family member have questions about hearing, we're here to provide information and support"

**Instead of:** "As recommended by the Australian Government"
**Write:** "We are a registered provider for government-funded hearing programs including the Hearing Services Program"

**Instead of:** "Brand X hearing aids are the best on the market"
**Write:** "We offer a range of hearing aid brands and styles to suit different needs and preferences"

**Instead of:** "You're too old to ignore your hearing loss any longer"
**Write:** "Hearing assessments are available for people of all ages who wish to understand their hearing health"

**Instead of:** Resharing a patient's Facebook post saying "I can hear my grandkids perfectly now!"
**Write:** Share educational content or service information only; do not amplify outcome claims

**Instead of:** Before/after photos showing "life with vs without hearing aids"
**Write:** Use neutral imagery that doesn't imply dramatic transformation or guaranteed outcomes

**Instead of:** "Don't let your elderly parent suffer in silence - bring them in today"
**Write:** "We're available to answer questions about hearing health for individuals and families"

**Instead of:** "Free hearing test! *conditions apply, minimum purchase required"
**Write:** "Complimentary initial consultation with no obligation - contact us for details"

**Instead of:** "Is your child falling behind at school? Their hearing could be the problem!"
**Write:** "Paediatric hearing assessments can help identify if hearing is a factor in learning - speak with your child's GP or teacher for guidance"
