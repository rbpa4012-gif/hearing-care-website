## HEARING HEALTH COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK - 22 RULES

### STRICTLY PROHIBITED CONTENT:

1. **Home Remedies & Self-Treatment** - No DIY ear cleaning, home remedies, or self-treatment advice
2. **Nutritional & Dietary Claims** - No food/supplement recommendations for hearing health
3. **Pricing & Dollar Values** - No specific prices, ranges, or percentage discounts
4. **Competitor References** - No naming or comparing competitors
5. **Unproven Health Condition Links** - No dementia/diabetes/heart disease causing hearing loss claims
6. **Testimonials & Reviews** - No outcome-based testimonials (AHPRA regulated)
7. **Qualifications & Credentials** - No exaggerated or false credentials
8. **Urgency & Fear-Based Marketing** - No scare tactics or artificial urgency
9. **Age & Disability Sensitivity** - No patronising or discriminatory language
10. **Third-Party Claims & Endorsements** - No fake endorsements or unauthorised logo use
11. **Product & Brand References** - No undisclosed brand bias or disparagement
12. **Social Media & User-Generated Content** - Must moderate outcome-based comments
13. **Before-and-After Imagery** - No transformation imagery implying outcomes
14. **Vulnerable Groups Protection** - No exploiting elderly or vulnerable individuals
15. **Inducements & Offers Transparency** - No hidden conditions on promotions
16. **Children & Paediatric Services** - No pressure tactics targeting parents
17. **"Invisible" Claims** - No claiming hearing aids are "invisible" unless truly invisible
18. **Free Test Clarification** - Must clarify if "free hearing test" is screening only vs full diagnostic assessment
19. **Telehealth Limitations** - Must clearly state availability and limitations of telehealth consultations
20. **Conflict of Interest Disclosure** - Must disclose any financial interests in hearing aid manufacturers
21. **Warranty Separation** - Must clearly separate device warranty from service warranty
22. **Government Program Accuracy** - Must accurately represent HSP, NDIS, DVA, Medicare eligibility and requirements

### PROHIBITED CLAIMS (from AHPRA/TGA/ACCC):

- "Guaranteed hearing improvement" (without assessment)
- "100% satisfaction guarantee" (without conditions)
- "Cure for hearing loss or tinnitus"
- "Prevents dementia" (without peer-reviewed evidence)
- "Instant or immediate results"
- "Risk-free treatment" (without disclaimers)
- "Reversal of hearing loss"
- "Comfortable" (requires specific substantiation)
- Medical device performance claims without TGA approval
- Emergency response time guarantees
- "Official partners" claims without authorisation

### HIGH-RISK TERMS (Avoid or Use Carefully):

| High Risk | Compliant Alternative |
|-----------|----------------------|
| comfortable | "designed for extended wear" or "ergonomically designed" |
| guarantee | "aim to" or "designed to help" |
| cure | "manage" or "may help improve" |
| 100% | "many patients" or "clinical studies show" |
| risk-free | "generally well-tolerated" with disclaimers |
| instant | "gradual adjustment period" or "results may vary" |
| reverse | "may help manage" or "designed to assist with" |
| best | Avoid comparative claims without evidence |
| proven | Requires specific evidence citation |
| revolutionary | Avoid innovation claims without substantiation |

### GENERAL DON'Ts:
- Make specific medical claims or guarantees
- Provide diagnosis or treatment recommendations
- Use terms like "cure," "heal," or "guaranteed results"
- Give specific medical advice
- Make assumptions about the reader's condition
- Use absolute language ("always," "never," "definitely," "will")
- Present opinions or possibilities as certainties

### COMPLIANT LANGUAGE PATTERNS:
- Use "may," "could," "some people find," "individual results vary"
- Direct readers to seek professional assessment
- Focus on education and empowerment, not fear
- Reference government programs by name without dollar amounts
- Use person-first language (e.g., "person with hearing loss")

## REGULATORY FRAMEWORK

This project must comply with:

### Primary Regulatory Bodies:
- **AHPRA** - Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency advertising guidelines
- **TGA** - Therapeutic Goods Administration (hearing aids are Class IIa medical devices)
- **ACCC** - Australian Competition and Consumer Commission consumer law
- **Australian Privacy Act** - Patient data and privacy requirements

### Professional Bodies:
- **Audiology Australia (AudA)** - Professional standards and ethics
- **Australian College of Audiology (ACAud)** - Including hearing aid audiometrists

### Government Programs:
- **Hearing Services Program (HSP)** - Government funding administration
- **NDIS** - National Disability Insurance Scheme
- **DVA** - Department of Veterans' Affairs hearing services
- **Medicare (MBS)** - Medicare Benefits Schedule items

### Australian Standards:
- AS/NZS 2344 - Hearing aids and hearing aid systems
- AS/NZS 60601.2.66 - Medical electrical equipment for hearing aids
- ISO 12871 - Hearing aids electromagnetic compatibility
- Privacy Act 1988 compliance
- Disability Discrimination Act 1992

### Penalties:
- **AHPRA breach**: Up to $5,000 individual / $10,000 corporate (criminal offence)
- **ACCC breach**: Fines up to millions, court proceedings, injunctions
- **TGA breach**: Product recalls, marketing suspension, penalties

## REQUIRED DISCLAIMERS

### Educational Content:
> "This article is for educational purposes only. Always consult with a qualified audiologist for personalised advice."

### Service Pages:
- Individual results may vary
- Professional hearing assessment required
- Hearing aids are Class IIa medical devices

### Pricing/Funding Content:
- Government funding eligibility conditions apply
- Contact us for current pricing information
- Costs may vary based on individual needs

### Device Content:
- TGA registered medical device
- Limitations of hearing aid technology
- Follow-up care requirements
- Device warranty and service warranty terms

### Free Services:
- Clearly state if screening only vs full diagnostic assessment
- State any conditions or limitations
